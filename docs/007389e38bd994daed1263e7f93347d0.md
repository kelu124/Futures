## Summary

George Clemenceau's insights reflect on the inherent challenges of achieving peace compared to the simplicity of war-making, emphasizing a bias toward action prevalent in contemporary decision-making, particularly influenced by AI's rapid advancements. He contrasts intuitive, fast decision-making (System One) with slower, reflective thinking (System Two), highlighting how overconfidence and organizational cultures often prioritize buoyant optimism over careful risk assessment. The author shares personal struggles with productivity, citing a recent injury and overwhelming task management as hindrances, while eventually finding clarity through task organization. The piece concludes with a glimpse into upcoming topics, including societal responses to the 'girl boss' phenomenon and the growing backlash against AI.
