## Summary

The intersection of nuclear technology and cybersecurity is becoming increasingly critical. The IAEA has expressed concerns over the integrity of the Chernobyl site following a drone strike, emphasizing risks in conflict zones. In the UK, the Sellafield facility faced cyber-attacks believed linked to Russia and China, raising alarms about national security infrastructure. Notably, countries like Sweden and Japan are advancing nuclear energy solutions, while the U.S. Air Force looks to implement microreactors for military use. Additionally, U.S. agencies are warning against potential cyber threats to various essential sectors. The geopolitical landscape is also changing with Western initiatives to secure communication infrastructure from foreign disruptions. This renewed focus on energy security, technological progress, and cybersecurity is reshaping international policies and responses.
