## Summary

The article explores the phenomenon of long queues outside eateries in London, particularly focusing on establishments like Swiss Butter, which draw crowds despite offering nothing unique. The waiting culture has turned into an integral part of the dining experience, driven partly by social media influencers who generate buzz around these establishments. Queues represent not just a means to access food but have become an attraction in themselves, embodying communal experience and social engagement. Notably, eateries like Dishoom have capitalized on this, with some speculating on the creation of artificial queues. While the popularity of long waits brings challenges for businesses in terms of management, there are also positive aspects, such as fostering community connections.
