## Summary

The article discusses the potential decline of dedicated graphics cards (GPUs) as integrated graphics (iGPUs) improve and become more viable for gaming. It acknowledges that while iGPUs are advancing rapidly, there remains a significant performance gap that ensures powerful GPUs will still be necessary for high-end gaming and professional industries for years to come. However, the author argues that the increasing capabilities of CPUs and AI technologies, like Nvidia's DLSS, threaten the GPU market. The piece reflects on the author's personal views and experiences with gaming hardware, while also considering the potential shift in focus from high-end GPUs to more integrated solutions in the future.
