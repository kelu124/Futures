## Summary

The article emphasizes the need for elasticity in the future, contrasting it with the outdated notion of 'plastics.' It suggests that individuals and organizations must adapt to the changing landscape of shopping, media consumption, and work, where traditional models like the purchase funnel have become obsolete. The future workforce will involve diverse, distributed, and flexible employment structures, making it essential for companies to rethink their strategies. Key strategies for developing elasticity include aligning with future trends, rethinking organizational structures, accelerating decision-making, utilizing data effectively, and maintaining core values while adapting. The article concludes by promoting a podcast episode that offers insights on developing leadership and relationship skills necessary for thriving in a rapidly changing world.
