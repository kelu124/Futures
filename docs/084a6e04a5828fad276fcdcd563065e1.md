## Summary

The convergence of technology and biology is paving new paths in design, health, and sustainability. Cyborg Botany, which combines electronic functions with plant biology, enables interactive plant devices. Regenerative design is pushing for ethical urban planning that prioritizes ecological health and social equity. Advances in synthetic biology, like DNA data storage and synthetic cells, raise safety concerns, necessitating caution. Wearable technology is becoming more integrated into daily life, while robotics, including biohybrid robots, show promise in healthcare. New antibiotics from soil samples and AI-designed proteins address antibiotic resistance and healthcare needs. Innovations in bioplastics underscore efforts to combat plastic waste. A range of proposed initiatives aims to integrate sustainability into multiple sectors, reflecting an urgent call for responsible ecological actions.
