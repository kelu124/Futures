## Summary

The text discusses the consequences of a fast-paced world where speed is prioritized over judgment, as articulated by Yonatan Touval. It introduces Stewart Brand's Pace Layers model, which categorizes civilization's elements into layers that operate at different speeds. The author appeals for a shift towards 'log-level' thinking, favoring simple, timestamped observations over complex theories in times of rapid change. The text also contrasts 'flow', a productive state achieved through challenge and engagement, with 'unflow', a passive state of disengagement caused by technology's design, leading to mindless consumption. To thrive despite societal acceleration, individuals are encouraged to embrace judgment over speed and maintain active engagement with their surroundings.
