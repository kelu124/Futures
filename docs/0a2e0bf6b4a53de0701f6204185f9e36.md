## Summary

This text provides a comprehensive list of chopsticks faux pas known as kiraibashi in Japanese dining etiquette. It details various improper uses of chopsticks, such as raising them too high, cleaning them in soup, passing food between pairs of chopsticks, holding bowls with chopsticks still in hand, and others. Each faux pas is listed in the Japanese syllabary order, along with its name in Japanese. It ranges from serious taboos to manners considered impolite, emphasizing the importance of following proper chopstick etiquette in Japanese culture. The glossary serves as an educational guide for individuals unfamiliar with these customs.
