## Summary

High-end homeowners are increasingly shifting towards low-tech living environments due to frustrations with the complexities and privacy concerns associated with smart home technologies. This trend reflects a desire for tactile experiences and self-sufficiency, moving away from digital conveniences. Privacy issues are prominent, particularly regarding smart devices like Amazon’s Echo and Ring, raising questions about data collection and surveillance. Concerns about security in smart technology are also highlighted, with vulnerabilities exposing potential hacking risks. While innovations in areas such as AI and energy management promise advancements, the need for ethical considerations and regulatory measures, especially regarding neurotechnology, remains crucial as society navigates the balance between innovation and privacy.
