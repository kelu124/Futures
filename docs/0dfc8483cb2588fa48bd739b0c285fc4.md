## Summary

This piece reflects on the interplay between history, literature, and contemporary issues, particularly in the context of AI and climate adaptation. It discusses how writers are using historical and literary frameworks to analyze modern anxieties and phenomena, drawing parallels to Mary Shelley’s Frankenstein and its exploration of humanity’s relationship with technology. The article by René Walter introduces the concept of 'skeleton libraries' in understanding Large Language Models (LLMs) as interpolatable archives, emphasizing their departure from traditional forms of literacy. Thomas Moynihan's speculation on humans potentially becoming 'Sun-eaters' connects energy consumption and civilization’s future to historical thought. Lastly, it touches on broader societal themes, including collective responses to crises and the evolving understanding of knowledge and information in the digital age, highlighting the complex landscape we navigate today.
