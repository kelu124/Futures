## Summary

The article discusses "The Uncomfortable," a project by Greek architect Katerina Kamprani that focuses on intentionally poorly designed everyday objects. The project aims to highlight the value of good design by showcasing impractical items, such as a fork with a chain handle. Kamprani's journey began after dropping out of a master's program and feeling that traditional architecture was too serious. Since its inception in 2011, her work has received recognition through exhibitions across Europe. She emphasizes that her goal is to amuse herself and express creativity rather than to produce commercially viable products. Kamprani also notes the importance of design for accessibility and how her project led to self-discovery, but she remains hesitant to mass-produce her creations to avoid shifting from artist to business owner.
