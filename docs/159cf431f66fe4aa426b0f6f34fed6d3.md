## Summary

Millennials are experiencing a resurgence of nostalgia as pop culture from their formative years, including concerts, films, and television, becomes prominent again. Events like Backstreet Boys concerts evoke deep feelings of escapism for this generation, allowing them to relive simpler times. Authors like Kate Kennedy highlight how nostalgia connects millennials, making them feel less isolated. Interestingly, even Gen Z is starting to romanticize the millennial era, leading to a revival of early 2000s music artists and Hollywood films. Classics like 'The Devil Wears Prada' are being revisited, confirming the enduring appeal of millennial culture as studios green-light sequels and reboots for modern audiences.
