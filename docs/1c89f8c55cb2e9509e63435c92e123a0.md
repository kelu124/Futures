## Summary

The article discusses the influence of video games on sports training, particularly highlighting NASCAR driver Ross Chastain's unconventional racing maneuver inspired by his childhood experiences with the game Nascar 2005. It emphasizes how video games, like Madden NFL and FIFA, provide athletes with tactical knowledge and skills, enhancing their understanding of game mechanics and strategy. The piece reflects on the evolution of sports training, noting that modern athletes have access to video games that can extend their training beyond physical practice. As the first generation of athletes who grew up with these games enters professional leagues, the impact of gaming on athletic performance is expected to grow significantly.
