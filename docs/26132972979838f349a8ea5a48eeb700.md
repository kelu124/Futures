## Summary

Willow, a new quantum chip from Google Quantum AI, marks a significant advancement in quantum computing. It achieves exponential error reduction as qubit numbers increase, addressing a long-standing challenge in quantum error correction. Notably, Willow completed a benchmark computation in under five minutes, a task that would take conventional supercomputers 10 septillion years. This performance underscores the potential of quantum computing, suggesting it can outperform classical systems as it scales up. Willow's development follows over a decade of research, and it aims to facilitate practical, commercially relevant applications of quantum technology. The chip is designed for high-quality performance, with a focus on integrated system engineering. Future goals include demonstrating useful computations relevant to real-world applications and leveraging quantum advancements to enhance AI capabilities.
