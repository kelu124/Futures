## Summary

This text explores the intersection of mass creativity and industrial production, questioning the role of human craftsmanship in manufacturing. The author contrasts traditional craftsmanship with automated industrial processes, emphasizing that the human touch remains crucial in many production scenarios, such as in aviation and artisanal food preparation. It reflects on the increasing customization in consumer products, particularly at Starbucks, where baristas face pressures from corporate demands and customer expectations for personalized drinks. The narrative highlights the complexities and challenges of maintaining quality and efficiency amidst these evolving demands. Ultimately, it contemplates the balance between mass production and the artistry of individual craftsmanship.
