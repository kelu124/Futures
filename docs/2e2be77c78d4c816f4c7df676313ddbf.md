## Summary

The text argues against the binary of optimism and pessimism regarding the future, proposing that both views assume a predetermined future. Instead, the author suggests embracing the idea of 'anti-dystopia' as a third way that acknowledges current crises without resorting to resignation. Utopias, although positive in vision, can be exclusive and unrealistic, while dystopian narratives foster defeatism. Anti-dystopia, as defined by Isabella Hermann, emphasizes action and agency in the face of adversity, encouraging individuals to create change despite uncertainty and imperfections. This approach values continuous efforts towards justice and community without expecting a perfect outcome, ultimately offering a liberating perspective that urges proactive engagement with the world's challenges.
