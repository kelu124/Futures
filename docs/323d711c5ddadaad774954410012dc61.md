## Summary

This text discusses the ongoing struggles of modern work culture, highlighting trends such as the '996 culture' in Silicon Valley where employees work extensive hours, including weekends, as a response to distractions during traditional work hours. It contrasts this with existential questions posed by philosopher Josef Pieper regarding the nature of work and human existence. Additionally, it introduces the concept of 'ungovernable change', where corporate change initiatives fail to engage employees effectively, leading to a lack of trust. The authors argue that successful companies must embrace continuous change as part of everyday processes and communicate effectively with their employees about the changing landscape of work.
