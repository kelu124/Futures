## Summary

Recent innovations in medical technology focus on less invasive procedures, improving patient care and outcomes. Techniques like Deep Tissue In Vivo Sound Printing (DISP) enable 3D printing of biocompatible structures inside the body, revolutionizing cancer therapy and regenerative medicine. The creation of a medical adhesive, 'Bone-02', offers quick solutions for bone fractures and reduces the need for invasive treatments. Concurrently, the integration of biological materials in robotics and electronics, including lifelike robotic skin and biohybrid robots, showcases advancements at the intersection of biology and technology. Sustainability initiatives emerge across fields, with biodegradable films and mushroom-derived electronics contributing to eco-friendly solutions. Health monitoring wearables are also evolving, and ethical concerns regarding biohacking and genetic engineering emerge. Research into advanced materials holds potential for energy generation and health applications while perceptions around tattoos reflect changing cultural attitudes toward self-expression.
