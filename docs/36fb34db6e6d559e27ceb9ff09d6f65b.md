## Summary

Apple's recent Worldwide Developers Conference highlighted significant advancements in its AI capabilities, focusing on a privacy-first, on-device approach. Key developments include built-in AI features enhancing native applications, a supercharged Siri acting as a meta-layer to automate tasks across devices, and improved ecosystem connectivity among Apple products. The new iPad calculator exemplifies the potential of AI with features like handwriting recognition and real-time graphing. Siri can now manage and summarize information across apps efficiently, presenting a competitive edge for Apple. Despite concerns over the feasibility of these innovations, if successful, they could revolutionize user interactions, reducing reliance on multiple applications. Additionally, Apple introduced a password manager and enhanced functionality for its Vision Pro headset, emphasizing ecosystem lock-in. Overall, while Apple faces criticism for late arrivals in certain features, its strategy positions it strongly in the AI landscape.
