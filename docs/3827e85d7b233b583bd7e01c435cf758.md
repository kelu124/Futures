## Summary

The article discusses the rapid advancements in generative AI technologies that allow individuals to create convincing deepfakes of themselves using just a photograph and short audio clips. The author shares their experience of generating a virtual version of themselves, which includes creating a realistic script with ChatGPT, generating a voice clone with ElevenLabs, and producing a video using D-ID. While these technologies are impressive and accessible, they also raise concerns about trust in digital media. The author highlights potential positive applications, such as AI avatars for customer support, but warns of the significant implications and challenges that come with this technology's proliferation.
