## Summary

The article explores the demographics of Middle-earth as analyzed by Emil Johanssen through the LOTR Project, highlighting significant gender imbalances in Tolkien's works, where 82% of characters are male. It discusses the rarity of female characters, particularly among races like Orcs and Dwarves, and examines the life spans of various races, revealing that Hobbits live an average of 96.8 years, while Dwarves can reach up to 340 years. The article also touches on the oldest and youngest characters in Tolkien's universe, noting the anecdotal nature of the data due to incomplete records. Johanssen's project includes detailed maps of character journeys and geographical features of Middle-earth, providing insights into the narratives of both Bilbo and Frodo.
