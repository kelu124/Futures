## Summary

China has overtaken the US in the adoption of OpenClaw, a framework for autonomous AI agents that automate enterprise workflows. Chinese tech giants are investing in lower-cost domestic AI models, creating a competitive ecosystem against Western technologies. While US companies have focused on perfecting AI models and safety protocols, Chinese enterprises rapidly implemented OpenClaw agents for various tasks, achieving mass adoption due to significantly lower costs—60-80% cheaper than Western alternatives. Unlike traditional chatbots, these AI agents can perform complex tasks autonomously, leading to rapid industry transformation. This shift has surprised Western observers, as US firms previously led in early implementations of OpenClaw, but China's supportive environment has facilitated explosive growth in AI deployment.
