## Summary

The text discusses the concept of "adjusting one's perspective," emphasizing that significant internal changes in perception are often imperceptible externally and occur naturally. The author reflects on a conversation about the future of AI and its implications for society, highlighting the bottlenecks of automating jobs and the potential need for universal basic income. It introduces the idea of "vivus skills"—attributes that cannot be automated or quantified, emphasizing the importance of deep attention and presence in life. The narrative references thinkers like Georges Perec and Annie Ernaux, advocating for a recognition of everyday existence and the subtle nuances of life that machines overlook. Ultimately, it calls for an expansion of our understanding of skills to encompass the depths of human experience and connection.
