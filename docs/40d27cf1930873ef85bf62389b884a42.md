## Summary

The author reflects on the implications of convenience culture, particularly in the context of AI and technology. Through references to Tim Wu and David Graeber, the piece critiques the modern obsession with frictionless experiences, arguing that true growth and understanding stem from discomfort and effort. The author emphasizes the importance of human connection, creativity, and the value of struggle, noting that meaningful art arises from personal investment and challenges. They express a philosophical opposition to generative AI, suggesting that art should reflect the complexity of human experience rather than be produced through easy means. Ultimately, the piece advocates for embracing friction as a path to authenticity and deeper engagement with life and community.
