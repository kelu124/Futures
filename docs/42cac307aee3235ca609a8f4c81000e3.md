## Summary

The article critiques the modern trend of escapism fostered by technology, arguing that it detracts from real human experiences and interactions. The author discusses how tech companies dehumanize users by promoting distraction and avoidance of life's inconveniences. As a solution, the concept of "friction-maxxing" is proposed, which involves intentionally embracing life's challenges and discomforts to foster resilience and enjoyment, particularly in parenting. The author shares personal anecdotes emphasizing the value of discomfort, such as a challenging family road trip that led to a love of reading. The article calls for a mindful approach to technology usage and a commitment to maintaining the friction necessary for personal growth and deeper connections.
