## Summary

In October 2024, Rachel Moore experienced a profound encounter with a humpback whale in French Polynesia, capturing stunning photos of the whale's eye. This beautiful interaction, however, ended tragically when the whale drowned after being struck by a boat shortly afterward. Moore's experience raised awareness and spurred legislative changes, leading to new speed regulations for large vessels during whale migration seasons in the region. The law mandates that boats over 12 meters must maintain specific speed limits within one nautical mile of the islands, aiming to prevent similar tragedies and protect humpback whales.
