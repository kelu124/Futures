## Summary

The text explores the traditional counting system 'yan tan tethera' used by shepherds across parts of the UK, along with its variations in dialects and origins. This system features distinctive names for numbers that derive from Brythonic Celtic languages, showcasing regional differences. The article includes a comparison chart of the counting terms in various dialects, highlighting their connection to Welsh numerals. The system is tied to cultural practices, such as counting sheep, and has appeared in various forms of art and music, notably in James Joyce's 'Finnegans Wake' and performances by folk artists like Jake Thackray. The discussion also touches on the etymology and mnemonic aspects of the counting terms, underscoring the system's historical significance and cultural resonance.
