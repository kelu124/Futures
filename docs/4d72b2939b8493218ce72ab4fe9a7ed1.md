## Summary

The text explores the significance of teeth in human evolution, culture, and personal history. It reflects on the dual nature of teeth as both a source of strength and vulnerability, highlighting the painful experiences associated with dental issues, particularly through personal anecdotes about the author's grandmother, Zosia, a dentist. The narrative weaves in historical context, touching upon the impact of war and political regimes on personal lives, as well as the medical and psychological implications of dental care. Ultimately, it emphasizes the idea that teeth are not just functional but also serve as a record of individual and collective memory, capturing experiences and histories through their physicality and condition.
