## Summary

In her piece "Roots and the Meaning of Life," Maria Popova reflects on the often overlooked significance of roots, not just in nature but as a metaphor for human existence. She draws on the work of botanist John Weaver, who meticulously studied root systems, highlighting how they mirror the complexity and character of above-ground life. Popova emphasizes that roots symbolize our hidden depths, suggesting that just as the invisible aspects of plants sustain them, our unseen qualities shape our identities. The piece is complemented by poetry from Hannah Fries, which echoes this theme of interconnectedness and the existential reflections that roots provoke.
