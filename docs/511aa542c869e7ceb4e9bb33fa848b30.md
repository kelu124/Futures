## Summary

The article discusses the unsettling rise of AI-generated images in real estate listings, which, while aimed at reducing costs for agents, often lead to disappointment among potential buyers when viewed in person. Many buyers feel a psychological disconnection from these images, which tend to fall into the 'uncanny valley'—almost realistic but ultimately unsettling. The author argues that homes embody personal aspirations and the distorting effect of AI on listings can trigger feelings of loss and disappointment. Despite some usage of AI in real estate, many agents like Kati Spaniak prefer traditional staging as the technology risks creating a disconnect between consumer expectations and reality, particularly in a time of economic stress. This sentiment raises broader questions about the impact of AI on human emotions and aspirations.
