## Summary

Leet, also known as leetspeak or 1337, is a form of modified language used primarily online that plays with characters and symbols to substitute letters in words, making them look unique and often obfuscated. The term derives from 'elite,' used in gaming and hacking cultures. Leet became popular in the 1980s on bulletin board systems as a way to gain elite status and bypass content filters. Over time, it has entered mainstream culture and inspired derivative forms like 'algospeak,' which helps users evade algorithmic censorship. Leet's defining characteristics include variable orthography, unique suffixes, and flexibility in grammar and vocabulary, often incorporating humor and cultural references from the online environment. While originally a hacker jargon, leet now includes various informal internet slangs and is recognized globally in various languages. It serves both as a playful means of communication and a tool for coded messages.
