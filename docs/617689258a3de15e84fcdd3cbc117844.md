## Summary

The text reflects on the author's experiences with Dungeons & Dragons (D&D) during high school and college, focusing on the importance of effective narrative in roleplaying games. It contrasts two styles of play: using miniatures versus 'theater of the mind,' where descriptions are verbal. The author shares insights from a Dragon magazine article emphasizing the need to prioritize important information in descriptions to enhance player engagement and understanding. The text discusses context, tone, and mood in storytelling and parallels these concepts with writing alt text for images, advocating for clarity and emotional resonance. The author encourages practice in both roleplaying and alt text writing to improve skills and create memorable experiences for participants.
