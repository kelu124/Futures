## Summary

The text delves into the concept of long-term thinking and legacy through various projects that aim to preserve knowledge and resources for future generations, such as the Future Library, the Ice Memory Foundation, and the Svalbard Global Seed Vault. It contrasts the idea of remembering through initiatives that expect future generations to appreciate them with the Onkalo project, which considers whether to conceal a repository for nuclear waste. The discussion highlights the balance between legacy and forgetting, emphasizing the value of being a 'good ancestor'— investing in the present for the benefit of those who will come after us, regardless of whether we will ever see the outcomes of our efforts. This reflection on curiosity versus caution in human behavior underscores the thoughtful approach needed for future sustainability.
