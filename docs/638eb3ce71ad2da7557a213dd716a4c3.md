## Summary

This essay examines the intricate relationship between technology and wealth, proposing three key metaphors: technology acts as a straw, siphon, and sieve. It argues that technology enables faster extraction of resources (the straw), concentrates wealth in the hands of a few (the siphon), and shifts ecological benefits towards humans at the expense of the natural world (the sieve). By re-evaluating wealth to mean usable energy and resources rather than just financial assets, the author suggests that uncritically celebrating technology may lead to ecological degradation and social inequality. The essay emphasizes the importance of understanding these dynamics to foster a more sustainable and equitable future, especially in the context of emerging technologies like AI, and raises critical questions about responsibility, progress, and the long-term consequences of our actions.
