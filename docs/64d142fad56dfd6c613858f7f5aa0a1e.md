## Summary

In 2017, Alexandra Rowland coined the term "hopepunk" as a counter to the grimdark genre of fantasy literature, which focuses on human cruelty. Hopepunk celebrates positivity and kindness as acts of rebellion against cynicism. The movement gained traction, with works like "The Lord of the Rings" and "The Handmaid's Tale" being identified as examples of hopepunk. Authors like Becky Chambers emphasize the importance of depicting hope amid adversity, showing characters who strive for goodness despite their flaws. The genre suggests that hope can be a sustaining force, encouraging readers to seek light in darkness, especially in challenging times, highlighting the necessity of optimism and resilience in an uncertain world.
