## Summary

Microsoft is launching an enterprise-level Copilot tool, enabling companies to create custom AI applications, including chatbots and agents for automating tasks. A closed beta has begun, with public testing scheduled for next month. Copilot Studio offers a low-code environment for developing AI assistants, aiming to automate tedious duties while allowing employees to focus on higher-level tasks. Notable companies like Thomson Reuters and McKinsey & Company have successfully implemented these agents, improving efficiency significantly. However, Salesforce's CEO, Marc Benioff, criticized Microsoft's Copilot for its inaccuracies and failure to meet customer expectations, suggesting concerns about its overall effectiveness and corporate intelligence capabilities.
