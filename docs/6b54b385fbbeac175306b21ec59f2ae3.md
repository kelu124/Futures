## Summary

The text explores the concept of 'propriety' as a guiding principle for businesses navigating the complexities of reality and authenticity in a polarized media landscape. It critiques the chase for radical authenticity and trend-driven approaches, suggesting instead that businesses cultivate a sommelier-like discipline towards understanding context and reality. This is achieved through three strategies: 1) cultivating synesthesia to engage multiple senses and grasp truths, 2) slowing down processes to allow for deeper appreciation and understanding, and 3) creating enclaves that encourage curiosity and engagement with reality. The authors emphasize the importance of storytelling and personal interactions in fostering resonance and value in business.
