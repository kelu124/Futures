## Summary

The article discusses advancements in AI capabilities, particularly the recent release of Claude-3, which achieved an IQ score above 100 for the first time. The author compares various AIs' performance on verbalized IQ tests, highlighting significant improvements in Claude's versions over time. Claude-3 stands out for its ability to accurately solve complex problems, indicating a leap in AI intelligence. The author emphasizes the importance of understanding AI capabilities for future societal impacts and suggests that while AI progress may pose existential risks, it could also lead to transformative benefits. The need for further monitoring and a cautious approach to AI development is emphasized, alongside personal recommendations for individuals in light of potential future changes due to AI advancements.
