## Summary

In February, the US economy saw an unexpected loss of 92,000 jobs, raising concerns about the labor market's stability. The unemployment rate increased to 4.4%, marking the largest job loss since October. Nearly every sector, including healthcare, experienced reductions in employment, while federal government jobs continued to decline. These figures have cast doubt on the recovery of hiring after a slowdown in 2025 and have led to negative reactions in the stock market. Although some officials predict strong job growth ahead, the situation complicates the Federal Reserve's decision-making, particularly regarding potential interest rate cuts due to rising oil prices.
