## Summary

Ken Burns' documentary on Leonardo da Vinci aims to dispel long-held misconceptions about the famed polymath's life and work. By emphasizing da Vinci's approach rooted in direct observation of nature rather than scholarly tradition, the film presents a more accurate portrayal of his contributions. The documentary explores how da Vinci's interdisciplinary interests combined art, science, and nature in ways that remain relevant today. Burns highlights the need for storytelling that respects the complexities and contradictions of history, paralleling da Vinci's own approach to understanding the world around him.
