## Summary

The article discusses the upcoming entry of Mickey Mouse into the public domain on January 1, 2024, following years of copyright extensions that many believed would prevent this day from ever arriving. It highlights the contradictory relationship Disney has with public domain and copyright laws, as the company has both thrived on public domain works while also lobbying for extended copyright protections. Jennifer Jenkins and James Boyle of the Duke Center for the Study of the Public Domain have celebrated this transition and provided clarity on what elements of Mickey Mouse can be used without infringing on copyrights or trademarks. The piece emphasizes the excitement surrounding this milestone and encourages creators to unleash their creativity with the public domain, underscoring the importance of public domain in inspiring new works.
