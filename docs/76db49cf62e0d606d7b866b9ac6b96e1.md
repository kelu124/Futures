## Summary

Michelle Higa Fox discusses the evolving role of creatives in an AI-driven landscape, emphasizing the importance of a generalist mindset over mere specialization in tools or techniques. Essential traits for longevity in creative fields include openness, curiosity, courage, and a commitment to learning. Rather than focusing solely on mastering new technologies, creatives should strengthen their foundational skills in storytelling and discernment. Fox illustrates how her journey from a specialized animator to a director involved not only technical mastery but also personal growth through diverse experiences, such as improv classes. Ultimately, she advocates for a broad-based approach to creativity, which acknowledges that growth can stem from seemingly unrelated disciplines and that true resilience in the face of AI lies in strong fundamental skills and interpersonal connections.
