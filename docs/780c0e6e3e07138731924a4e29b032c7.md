## Summary

The intersection of artificial intelligence (AI) and neuroscience is witnessing rapid advancements that enhance human cognition and understanding of brain processes. Researchers are employing AI to decode brain activity and develop brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) and biological computing systems, like the CL1 biological computer and DishBrain chip, to improve communication abilities and develop new treatments. AI's application in design is transforming productivity through innovative tools like Autodesk’s neural CAD models. However, ethical concerns accompany these advancements, mainly around AI biases and the implications of neurotechnology on privacy and autonomy. The evolving landscape emphasizes the need for regulatory frameworks, especially as technology integrates into healthcare and workplace settings, fostering a culture of neurodiversity and collaboration.
