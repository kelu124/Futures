## Summary

The ongoing conflict in the Middle East is disrupting supplies and increasing prices for essential raw materials like aluminum and helium, affecting Korean manufacturers, particularly in the electronics and semiconductor sectors. Aluminum prices have surged due to fears of supply disruptions linked to threats from Iran regarding the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial shipping route. This region produces 9% of global aluminum, with significant exports to Europe and the U.S. Disruptions may push prices beyond $4,000 per ton, impacting companies like Samsung and LG. The semiconductor industry is also at risk, particularly due to helium supply issues from Qatar, which supplies 64.7% of South Korea's helium imports. Although short-term impacts may be managed through diversified supply chains, a prolonged conflict could strain resources significantly.
