## Summary

This piece discusses the historical significance and evolution of the "Childcraft – The How and Why Library," an encyclopedia series for children created in 1934, which reached 15 volumes by the 1950s. The author highlights a transition in illustrations from black-and-white to color during the 1960s and 1970s, focusing on the captivating prehistoric animal artwork. Notable artists like Alex Ebel and Rod Ruth contributed to these volumes, which resonated with kids, particularly Gen Xers, who found a special connection to dinosaurs and sci-fi themes. The article also touches on related educational literature from the era and links to external resources about vintage media and culture.
