## Summary

The text reflects on the significance of spreadsheets in modern society, highlighting their capacity for simulation and prediction. It references a 1984 article by Steven Levy that discusses how spreadsheets allow users to experiment with data and create models, providing a sense of control over complex systems. The author shares personal experiences during the Covid pandemic, where a spreadsheet helped clarify safety concerns regarding virus spread. Additionally, it mentions the broader applicability of spreadsheets across various fields and their role as tools for thinking about the future. The text concludes with recommendations for interesting readings and topics, including a review of Philip Ball's book on biology and discussions on technology, forecasting, and historical figures in computing.
