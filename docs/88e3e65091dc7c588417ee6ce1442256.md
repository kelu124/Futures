## Summary

The article discusses the concept of 'storification' in technology and business, emphasizing how storytelling can be both a powerful tool and a potential risk. It highlights the successful storytelling strategies of figures like Steve Jobs and companies like Apple, which have propelled their products and brand identity. However, it warns of the dangers of over-reliance on narratives, as seen in the cases of Elon Musk's Twitter acquisition and the collapse of FTX, where simplistic stories obscured deeper issues. The author argues for a balanced approach to storytelling, recognizing its importance in entrepreneurship while advocating for scrutiny and critical analysis to differentiate between compelling narratives and reality.
