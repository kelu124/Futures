## Summary

In October 2023, around 70 children participated in a simulation exercise in Paris to rehearse responses to extreme heat scenarios projected for the future. The event, called "Paris at 50 degrees Celsius," aimed to prepare the city for potential temperatures of 122°F by 2100. The simulation included role-playing the effects of heat on health, food safety, and emergency services in an effort to improve preparedness among city officials and residents. With climate change threatening cities worldwide, similar drills are becoming more common. The exercise produced 50 recommendations for enhancing the city's crisis response and underlined the necessity of preparing not just officials but also local communities for future heat waves by offering training and resources to avoid severe impacts.
