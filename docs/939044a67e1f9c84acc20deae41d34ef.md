## Summary

Neurosecurity is a burgeoning field that focuses on protecting neural devices to ensure mental privacy and autonomy, likened to antivirus software for computers. Advances in brain-computer interfaces (BCIs) are transforming assistive technology, enabling individuals like Nancy Smith to regain lost skills. Non-invasive BCI technologies are being explored for treating neurological disorders. Innovations in neural signal measurement could revolutionize medical technologies. The interplay between AI and neuroscience is raising ethical concerns, especially related to mind-reading technologies and cognitive freedom. Researchers are also developing methods to enhance human capabilities through robotic limbs and the integration of biohybrid systems. Despite technological advancements, concerns about data privacy, job displacement, and unequal access persist, emphasizing the need for ethical considerations as society adapts to these innovations.
