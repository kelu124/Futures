## Summary

The digital age poses challenges for data preservation, risking a 'Digital Dark Age' due to hardware and format issues. Rural areas, like Tyurkmen in Bulgaria, face population decline, impacting ecological dynamics. Aesthetic norms and restrictions affect socioeconomic structures and climate action. The influence of technology ignites a call for deeper societal engagement, with polarization necessitating a focus on attention over availability. The rise of private power threatens democracy, while creativity evolves as a cultural mantra. Trends include reviving traditional values, promoting AI in cultural preservation, and recognizing cultural heritage's dynamic nature. The importance of community-driven digital preservation is emphasized, alongside the need for equitable land reform, enhanced public engagement, and the challenges posed to cultural heritage by environmental changes. Overall, there is a growing recognition of the interconnectedness of culture, technology, and ecological sustainability.
