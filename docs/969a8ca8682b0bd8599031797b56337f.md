## Summary

Wifi services at 19 UK train stations, including major hubs like London Euston and Manchester Piccadilly, were suspended following a cybersecurity incident. Passengers reported being redirected to a webpage with Islamophobic content and references to terrorist attacks. The incident was linked to an unauthorized change made from a legitimate administrator account, prompting an investigation by British Transport Police and Telent, the service provider. A Global Reach employee was arrested under computer misuse laws. This incident follows a recent cyber-attack on Transport for London (TfL), indicating a broader trend of cyber threats affecting public services.
