## Summary

Businesses in China are increasingly investing in virtual employees, with Baidu reporting a doubling of projects since 2021. These virtual beings, which integrate animation, sound tech, and machine learning, can perform various tasks and are gaining popularity across industries like finance and tourism. Costs for creating virtual people have dropped significantly, and the market is expected to grow by 50% annually through 2025. China's government is also promoting the virtual person industry, aiming for a valuation of over 50 billion yuan by 2025. Brands are turning to virtual influencers to avoid scandals associated with celebrities, with a significant portion of consumers engaging with these digital personas. Major tech companies like Bilibili and Tencent are pioneering developments in this space, alongside emerging startups.
