## Summary

The author reflects on the declining drinking culture in America, contrasting anecdotes of carefree drunkenness with the current reality of moderation and hangovers. Since her youth, the author has transitioned to moderate drinking, influenced by changing societal attitudes towards alcohol, as evidenced by drops in consumption and government warnings about its risks. Despite this shift, she expresses nostalgia for the vivacious drinking culture that inspired writers, noting how literature has evolved from indulgent tales of inebriation to more serious depictions of alcohol's consequences. The essay highlights F. Scott Fitzgerald's writings on drinking, showcasing his complex relationship with alcohol and its impact on his artistry, while also touching on recent developments suggesting a reconsideration of drinking guidelines.
