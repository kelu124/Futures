## Summary

Bombay Beach, once a vibrant resort, has transformed into a surrealist art hub amid ecological decline. As the Salton Sea shrinks and pollution rises, artists have revitalized the town through the Bombay Beach Biennale, creating installations and fostering community. Despite challenges, including tensions between new artists and long-time residents, the Biennale has sparked a reimagining of the town's identity, highlighting resilience and mutual care. The article explores how ruins can inspire creativity and community, showcasing Bombay Beach as a model for fostering supportive futures even in the face of adversity.
