## Summary

The Digital Regulation Handbook is a comprehensive resource developed by the World Bank and the International Telecommunication Union to guide countries in digital regulation amidst ongoing technological advancements. It addresses critical questions about protecting digital infrastructure, ensuring affordable internet access, safeguarding personal data, and adapting regulations to new technologies. The Handbook provides actionable insights on various topics, including regulatory governance, competition, consumer rights, and emerging technologies, and is available in multiple languages. Accompanied by an Online Platform for updated guidelines, it aims to support decision-makers, academics, and the general public in navigating the challenges of digital transformation, especially in the wake of increased cybersecurity threats and reliance on digital technologies highlighted by the COVID-19 pandemic.
