## Summary

The UK government has announced a £279 million investment to enhance the Army’s cyber capabilities at Duke of Gloucester Barracks in Gloucestershire. This project will create modern accommodation and training facilities for the 13 Signal Regiment, which conducts defensive cyber operations. Key developments include 248 Single Living Accommodation rooms and 30 Service Family Accommodation homes, aimed at improving the living conditions of service personnel and their families. The initiative is expected to generate 92 jobs, including apprenticeships, and emphasizes the defence sector's role in local economic growth. Construction is scheduled to begin in Summer 2027, aiming for completion by Spring 2030, reinforcing the UK’s commitment to advanced cyber operations amid increasing cyber threats.
