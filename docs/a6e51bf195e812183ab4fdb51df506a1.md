## Summary

Global military spending has hit a record $2440 billion in 2023, marking a significant 6.8% increase from 2022, the highest rise in a decade, according to the Stockholm International Peace Research Institute (Sipri). This increase is observed across all five global regions and is driven by geopolitical tensions. The US and China account for nearly half of the total spending, with the US focusing on advanced military technologies. Russia's military budget surged by 24% amid ongoing conflict in Ukraine, while Ukraine's expenditure rose by 51%. Other countries, including India and Saudi Arabia, also reported increased military spending. The trend highlights a global shift toward prioritizing military strength, raising concerns about potential escalations in conflict.
