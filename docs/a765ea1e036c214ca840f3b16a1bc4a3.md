## Summary

The article discusses the 'Sing Wild Seeds' project, a collaboration between the Joseph Rowntree Foundation and Earth/Percent, led by Brian Eno. This initiative aims to engage musicians and audiences in co-creating music that envisions preferred futures, blending music-making with experiential futures to enhance public imagination. The project facilitates participatory processes where musicians develop unique performances and songs based on future scenarios, exploring themes like social justice and environmental sustainability. Initial workshops utilized creative exercises to inspire songwriting, resulting in imaginative compositions performed at events like Glastonbury Festival. The project seeks to cultivate a cultural capacity for collective dreaming and shaping of future possibilities through music.
