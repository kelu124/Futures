## Summary

The article reflects on the profound impact of Garry Winogrand's photography, particularly his ability to capture the beauty and humanity of ordinary people. The author shares personal experiences of feeling alienated from beauty in faith and how Winogrand's work transformed that perception, inviting them into a world where everyone belongs. The exhibit at the Brooklyn Museum serves as a secular sanctuary, depicting everyday life with vibrancy and warmth. Through recurring visits, the author finds solace and connection amid their own struggles, recognizing the sacredness in shared human experiences. The photographs reveal the complex interplay of solitude and community, echoing themes of longing and intimacy while highlighting the inherent beauty in the mundane.
