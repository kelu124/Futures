## Summary

The shift from bundled pricing to metered pricing in AI usage has implications for market expansion and user behavior. Previously, AI companies offered bundled access resembling gym memberships, encouraging broad usage without significant costs. However, as companies implement usage caps and metered pricing, they may face varied impacts on market size. Bundles work best with low marginal costs, while metered pricing accounts for real variable costs of AI services. The experience of early Internet advertising, which transitioned to a metered model, shows that pay-per-use can stimulate market growth and profitability, suggesting a similar evolution may occur in the AI sector.
