## Summary

The author reflects on their four-year hiatus from the consulting business, during which they focused on personal life changes and were less active in the Futures field. Despite a gap in their contributions, they express concern about the contemporary understanding of Futures, emphasizing the importance of recognizing historical foundations and contributions to avoid superficial interpretations. They critique the tendency of newer practitioners to recycle existing ideas without proper acknowledgment, particularly referencing the Futures Cone developed by Joe Voros. The article urges current and future professionals to explore the history of tools and methodologies in Futures, to understand their origins and importance, ensuring respectful citation and deeper comprehension of the field's evolution.
