## Summary

A new self-driving lab developed by researchers at North Carolina State University is revolutionizing material discovery by using dynamic flow experiments to generate data 10 times faster than traditional methods. This automated laboratory employs machine learning and real-time monitoring, allowing continuous experimentation and rapid data collection. As a result, researchers can make informed decisions about subsequent experiments almost instantly, fostering faster breakthroughs in crucial areas such as clean energy and sustainability while minimizing chemical use and waste. This innovative approach promises to significantly reduce the time and resources required for material discovery, leading to quicker solutions to societal challenges.
