## Summary

Gold and silver prices experienced significant declines following President Trump's nomination of Kevin Warsh as the chair of the Federal Reserve, which alleviated concerns about the Fed's independence and boosted the U.S. dollar. Spot silver fell 28% to $83.45 an ounce, while silver futures dropped 31.4% to $78.53, marking their worst performance since March 1980. Gold prices also plummeted around 9%. Analysts attribute these drops to profit-taking among investors and a stronger dollar, making metals more expensive for foreign buyers. Changes in market sentiment regarding the Fed's direction and geopolitical tensions contributed to recent fluctuations in precious metal prices, leading to a reassessment of investment risks in these assets.
