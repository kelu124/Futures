## Summary

Business schools are increasingly integrating AI into their curriculums to enhance graduates' competitiveness in the job market. Institutions like American University's Kogod School of Business plan to incorporate AI across their entire curriculum, offering 20 new classes, while Columbia's Hitendra Wadhwa has developed an AI-powered tool, LiFT, to aid students in leadership preparation. This tool personalizes coaching experiences and has shown promising results in improving students' decision-making and open-mindedness. Employers are prioritizing technology skills, with 75% indicating the importance of AI and machine learning competencies. However, many believe graduates are not sufficiently prepared. Professors also emphasize understanding AI's broader implications for the future of work, with varying approaches to student engagement and the emotional challenges posed by AI knowledge.
