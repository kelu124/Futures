## Summary

The book "Writings of the Luddites," edited by Kevin Binfield, compiles texts from the Luddite movement between 1811 and 1816, highlighting their literary and social resistance against mechanization in the textile industry. The Luddites, named after the legendary leader Ned Ludd, protested against the rise of machines, which they believed led to unemployment and poverty. This anthology includes poems, proclamations, and letters from various regions in England, complemented by an extensive introduction that contextualizes their rhetoric and historical significance. The work serves as a valuable resource for scholars of literature, labor history, and social movements, providing insights into a pivotal moment in industrial history.
