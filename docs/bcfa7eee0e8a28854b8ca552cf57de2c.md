## Summary

In the discussion of decision-making, the author emphasizes the importance of balancing autonomy and speed. Fast decisions can lead to negative outcomes, while slower, more thoughtful processes promote better choices. The essay reflects on the author's journey through different decision-making paradigms, specifically contrasting autocracy, consensus, consent, voting, and the advice process. Each method has its advantages and limitations, with the advice process allowing for greater autonomy while still incorporating input from others. The author advocates for the use of consent as a universally applicable approach due to its relatively quick and democratic nature. He warns against over-relying on autocracy or consensus and suggests these should be used sparingly, reserved for low-stakes decisions or small teams. Understanding these methods can empower organizations to make more informed and effective decisions.
