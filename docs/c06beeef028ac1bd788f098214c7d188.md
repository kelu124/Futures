## Summary

The article critiques the use of AI, particularly large language models (LLMs), in personal communication and relationships. It highlights concerns about etiquette, connection, and the essence of friendship being sacrificed for efficiency. The author argues that while AI can streamline tasks, it undermines the meaningful effort involved in personal interactions, such as writing heartfelt messages or eulogies. The distinction between exchanging information and forming connections is emphasized, suggesting that genuine communication requires personal engagement and care. Ultimately, the article contends that while AI may save time, it detracts from the emotional depth and experience of relationships.
