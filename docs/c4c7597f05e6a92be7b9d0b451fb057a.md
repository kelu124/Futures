## Summary

Urban development is shifting towards sustainability and resilience, with cities adopting innovative solutions to address environmental challenges. Nature-based Solutions (NbS) are particularly popular in locations like Scotland, aiming to enhance air quality and reduce emissions. The concept of tiny forests in Australia aims to tackle urban heat and promote community engagement. Cities are evolving their public spaces to be more inclusive, especially for children, fostering healthier environments. The future of work is leaning towards sustainable economies and circular models, creating new job opportunities. Technological advancements, including quantum sensing and Augmented Reality (AR), are crucial for urban management. The construction industry is focusing on sustainable practices and adaptive reuse of existing buildings. The United Nations’ “Summit of the Future” aims to unite global leaders to tackle climate change and poverty, yet political barriers persist, indicating a need for a comprehensive global strategy.
