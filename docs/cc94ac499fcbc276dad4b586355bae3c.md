## Summary

The integration of technology and healthcare is advancing significantly, with innovations in artificial intelligence (AI) improving diagnosis, treatment, and patient experience. UC Davis Health is piloting a study to assess AI in mammogram imaging to enhance breast cancer detection and lessen patient anxiety. AI is also improving diagnostic accuracy outside imaging, as seen in a Canadian early warning system that has successfully alerted healthcare providers to patients at risk. Innovative devices like DermaSensor and research into non-invasive cancer detection methods are gaining momentum, while accessible cancer treatment solutions and advancements in surgery are also emerging. Despite the benefits, ethical concerns regarding AI misuse, healthcare access disparities, and genetic discrimination persist, highlighting the need for careful implementation. Overall, ongoing technological advancements promise to reshape healthcare but require attention to ethical, reliable, and equitable practices.
