## Summary

The text discusses the importance and methodology of strategy through the lens of Wardley Mapping, a tool for visualizing strategic landscapes. It provides insights inspired by historical strategists like Sun Tzu, John Boyd, and Simon Wardley, addressing fundamental questions about strategy—its necessity and execution. The collection features a diverse array of Wardley Maps from various domains, demonstrating applications in core banking, remote work, product development, and more. Each map serves as an example to inspire others to adopt visual strategy in their own contexts. The text also includes a summary of key strategic concepts and challenges, emphasizing resilience, creativity, and adaptability in strategy formulation.
