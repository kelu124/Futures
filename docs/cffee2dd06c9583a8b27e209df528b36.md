## Summary

The article discusses the slow adoption of artificial intelligence (AI) in businesses despite executives expressing enthusiasm about its potential applications. Many companies, including JPMorgan and Yum! Brands, have identified numerous use cases for AI, yet the technology is not spreading as rapidly as expected. The article suggests that economic factors may play a role in this slow integration, hinting at broader implications for industries and the economy as a whole. It highlights that while many companies are excited about AI's possibilities, the actual implementation and widespread use remain sluggish.
