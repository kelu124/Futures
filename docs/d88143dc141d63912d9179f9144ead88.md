## Summary

FBI Director Kash Patel faced backlash after discussing the legality of firearm possession at protests following the shooting of Alex Pretti by immigration officers. His assertion that loaded firearms shouldn't be brought to protests was challenged by Second Amendment advocates, highlighting selective enforcement during previous protests. Subsequently, Patel announced an investigation into Signal chats used by Minnesotans for sharing federal immigration agents' movements, raising concerns over free speech. The Trump administration's history with protests, often dismissive of protesters' rights, mirrors Patel's actions as communities organize to resist federal immigration activities, creating channels for coordination among locals.
