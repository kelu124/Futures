## Summary

This newsletter reflects on Georges Perec's 50-year-old book "An Attempt to Exhaust a Place in Paris," which explores observation in urban settings, specifically the Place Saint-Sulpice. The author attempts a similar exercise in the same location to enhance their attention span, documenting details of the surroundings. The article also includes commentary on contemporary architecture, recent events regarding Sotheby’s takeover of the Breuer building, and The Louvre's renovation plans post-break-in. It touches upon a controversial interview from Architectural Record with David Adjaye, highlighting issues of journalistic integrity and access to architectural criticism. Ultimately, the piece emphasizes the importance of deep observation as a counter to modern distractions and the implications for architecture criticism.
