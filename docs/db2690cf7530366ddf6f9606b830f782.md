## Summary

The article discusses the emerging field of digital bioacoustics, which utilizes advanced technology to listen to previously inaudible sounds from plants and animals. By employing specialized microphones and AI software, scientists can record and analyze the communication patterns of various species, enhancing our understanding of their needs and behaviors. This research reveals that plants can communicate distress signals, such as dehydration, while also decoding the sounds of animals like elephants and whales. The potential for global acoustic monitoring programs could significantly aid conservation efforts and provide insights into species responses to climate change. As technology advances, the possibility of understanding plant communication may become a reality, offering new opportunities for human intervention and ecological awareness.
