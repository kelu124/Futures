## Summary

The author recounts their transformative experience of flying in the cockpit of a British Airways Boeing 747, which ignited their passion for aviation and the unique language of pilots, referred to as Aeroese. This specialized language, rich with nautical roots, facilitates global communication among pilots and air traffic controllers, emphasizing the importance of clarity and brevity. The author reflects on how English, or Aeroese, serves as a common linguistic bridge in international skies. They also explore the technical jargon, acronyms, and the blend of romance and precision inherent in aviation language, ultimately celebrating the beauty and complexity of flight and its lexicon.
