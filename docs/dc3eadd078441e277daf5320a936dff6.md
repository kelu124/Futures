## Summary

Vernor Vinge (1944â€“2024) was an influential American science fiction author and professor known for his contributions to the concepts of technological singularity and cyberspace. He earned his PhD in mathematics from the University of California, San Diego, and published numerous acclaimed works, winning multiple Hugo Awards for novels like 'A Fire Upon the Deep' and 'Rainbows End.' Vinge began his writing career in the 1960s, gaining prominence with his novella 'True Names' in 1981. His stories often explored innovative technological themes and their societal implications. After retiring from teaching in 2000, Vinge focused on writing until his death due to Parkinson's disease in 2024. His legacy continues to influence the science fiction genre.
