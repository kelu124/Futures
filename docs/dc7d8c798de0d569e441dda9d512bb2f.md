## Summary

A significant early-season heatwave is affecting the southwestern U.S., with temperatures rising 11-17℃ (20-30℉) above normal due to a strong high-pressure system. Forecasts predict Phoenix could reach temperatures of around 41.1℃ (106℉), breaking previous March records. This unprecedented heat presents severe public health risks, particularly for vulnerable populations unaccustomed to such temperatures. The heatwave could also have environmental repercussions by accelerating snowmelt and reducing summer water availability, increasing the risks of drought and wildfires. Extreme heat events are becoming more common and intense due to climate change, with current data suggesting a significant increase in their likelihood and intensity since pre-industrial times. Urban areas face heightened heat risks, necessitating urgent action and planning to address the issue more effectively, particularly for disadvantaged communities.
