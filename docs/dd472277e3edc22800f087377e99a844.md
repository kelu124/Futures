## Summary

In this text, the author discusses their simple data strategy framework that they have used in their experience as a data strategist. The framework is shaped as a pyramid, with the mission and vision at the top, followed by use cases, and supported by capabilities at the bottom. The author emphasizes the importance of having a clear data strategy that aligns with the overall strategy of the organization. They also highlight the need for specific data-driven use cases and the identification of the required data assets. The author stresses the interdependence of people, process, and technology in implementing the strategy and highlights the importance of having the right people, defining roles and responsibilities, and fostering a data-driven culture. The author also mentions the critical processes, such as governance and stakeholder management, and the relevant technologies, including architecture and operational aspects. The framework provides a structured approach for analyzing and answering data strategy questions.
