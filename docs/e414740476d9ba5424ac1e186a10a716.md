## Summary

The Animals in That Country is a 2020 novel by Laura Jean McKay that explores communication between animals and humans during a pandemic. Inspired by McKay's own experience with a virus during a writer's festival, the book presents a humorous and insightful examination of consciousness and language limits. It has garnered significant acclaim, winning multiple literary awards, including the Aurealis Award and the Arthur C. Clarke Award. Reviews highlight its blend of dark humor and serious themes, making it a notable debut in science fiction. The novel's release coincided with the COVID-19 pandemic, adding a layer of irony to its themes of global isolation and communication.
