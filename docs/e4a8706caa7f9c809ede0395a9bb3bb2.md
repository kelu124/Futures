## Summary

The article discusses the challenges of finding reliable travel recommendations online amidst a sea of sponsored listings and competing opinions. The author, who prides herself on meticulous travel planning, finds that traditional online sources are often diluted and unreliable. After a less-than-ideal experience in Devon, England, she discovers that genuine insights from locals, such as a chef at her bed and breakfast, yield far better results than her previous online research. She contrasts past travel publications that offered curated recommendations with the current landscape dominated by algorithms and social media, which often compromise authenticity. In conclusion, the author emphasizes the value of personalized human insights and shares techniques for finding trustworthy recommendations online despite these challenges.
