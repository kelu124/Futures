## Summary

The article argues that companies should reconsider their approach to AI, viewing it not as an autonomous coworker but as an exoskeleton that amplifies human capabilities. It presents real-world examples where exoskeleton technology has significantly enhanced productivity and reduced injuries in manufacturing, military, and medical sectors. Instead of aiming for AI systems that operate independently, the author suggests a micro-agent architecture where AI augments human judgment in decision-making processes. This model combines automated insights with human context, leading to improved efficiency and creativity in workflows. The article emphasizes that successful AI integration lies in supporting workers by alleviating repetitive and error-prone tasks rather than attempting full autonomy.
