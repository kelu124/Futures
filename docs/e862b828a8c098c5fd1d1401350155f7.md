## Summary

The article discusses the growing importance of human interaction in various fields as a counter to the rise of AI and technology. It highlights The Alabama Booksmith, an independent bookstore thriving by offering signed books and fostering personal connections between authors and customers. This trend reflects a broader societal shift where people prefer human touch and service over automated experiences, seen in the resurgence of live music, media, and even grocery shopping. The piece emphasizes the emerging roles of curators, concierges, and caregivers as essential in the digital age, suggesting that a return to authentic human experiences is both a new economy and a path to happiness.
