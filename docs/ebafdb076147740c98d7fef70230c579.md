## Summary

The article discusses the rising oligopoly in pop culture, where a few major players dominate various media, leading to a prevalence of remakes and a decline in original content. It reflects on Gen Z's fashion trends, the impact of AI on creativity, and cultural shifts towards hyper-personalization and consumer identity. The decline of quality in social media, called 'enshittification', points to the need for renewed connections among users. Additionally, societal changes by 2040, including challenges of sustainability, cultural fragmentation, and branding crises, are examined. New cultural movements like cozy lit offer comfort but risk commodification. The text concludes by emphasizing the need for innovative approaches to maintain diversity and creativity amidst technological advancements and societal changes.
