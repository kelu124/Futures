## Summary

The text revolves around the concept of "hanging out" and its decline in modern society. The author discusses their encounter with Sheila Liming, the author of a book titled "Hanging Out: The Radical Power of Killing Time." The author agrees with Liming's view that hanging out has become a "quiet catastrophe" due to factors like the pandemic and changing social norms. They reflect on their own experiences of hanging out with friends and how it has become more challenging in recent times. The text also explores the impact of technology on socializing and the importance of fostering genuine connections and shared experiences.
