## Summary

Three Scottish brothers, Ewan, Jamie, and Lachlan Maclean, have set a new world record for the fastest unsupported row across the Pacific Ocean, completing over 14,000 kilometers from Lima, Peru, to Cairns, Australia, in just 139 days. They raised over Â£795,000 for clean water projects in Madagascar during their journey, which faced numerous challenges including bad weather and technical difficulties. Despite battling seasickness and a near-fatal incident where Lachlan fell overboard, the brothers persevered. Their arrival in Cairns was marked by celebrations with family and friends. Jamie expressed newfound appreciation for everyday comforts after their adventure at sea.
