## Summary

Research conducted by scientists at the University of Utah found that lead concentrations in human hair have decreased by 100-fold over the past century, highlighting the effectiveness of EPA regulations on lead-based products, such as lead paint and gasoline, initiated in the 1970s. These regulations were crucial in mitigating the toxic impacts of lead exposure, a danger recognized since ancient times. The study also addresses concerns regarding potential deregulation by the Trump administration that could affect current lead regulations, particularly related to replacing old lead pipes in water systems. The findings serve as a reminder of the importance of environmental regulations, following the controversial history of leaded gasoline promoted by Thomas Midgley Jr., an advocate for conduits of industry despite the clear health risks involved.
