## Summary

The author reflects on the unpredictable nature of life and the value of studying history, which reveals that many had tougher experiences than present-day individuals. They note that unconventional life paths, often driven by obsessive interests, can lead to unique and fulfilling outcomes, even if they diverge from traditional career paths. The message emphasizes that the only essential rules for work are to be able to pay rent and abide by laws, allowing for personal fulfillment in diverse ways. Ultimately, the author concludes that there is no singular way to lead a meaningful life, and that varied experiences are part of the human condition.
