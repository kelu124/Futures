## Summary

Google has partnered with nuclear startup Kairos Power to secure carbon-free electricity for its AI data centers, aiming for an initial delivery of 500MW by 2030 and full deployment by 2035. This initiative comes amidst increasing power demands from tech companies, prompting discussions with the White House about energy availability. Other tech giants like Oracle and Microsoft are also exploring nuclear power options, with Oracle planning three small modular reactors and Microsoft investing in the Three Mile Island reactor. Despite some skepticism regarding the AI industry's sustainability, the push for innovative energy solutions continues as demand for power grows.
