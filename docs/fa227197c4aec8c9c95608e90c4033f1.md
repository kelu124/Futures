## Summary

Historian Jon Peterson discusses the history of wargames and their evolution into tabletop role-playing games (RPGs) in a detailed interview. Peterson highlights key contributors to wargaming, notably Georg von Reisswitz and his son, who were instrumental in developing modern wargames in the early 19th century, introducing dice and umpiring into gameplay to enhance realism. The conversation touches on the impact of technological advancements, such as telegraphs and nuclear weaponry, on game design, contrasting military-focused games and hobbyist pursuits like Dungeons & Dragons, which emerged during the anti-war sentiment of the Vietnam War. Peterson also notes the cyclical nature of game mechanics and debates about player agency versus rule adherence, and how these principles continue to influence modern gaming, including military simulations and games addressing advanced AI scenarios.
