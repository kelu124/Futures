## Summary

The author reflects on the significance of everyday photography, driven by a personal desire to capture moments for future generations, despite the likelihood that descendants may not care about them. He explores the challenges of preserving photographs, both digitally and physically, and the nature of what constitutes meaningful imagery. Through his experiences as a legally blind photographer, he emphasizes how mundane photos can reveal the rhythms of existence and offer a deeper understanding of the world. The piece also contemplates the absurdity of attempting to document life, yet finds value in the process of capturing and cherishing moments, suggesting that these efforts can lead to self-discovery and appreciation of life’s fleeting beauty.
