## Summary

A study by Common Sense Media reveals that 72% of U.S. teens have tried AI companions, primarily chatbots for personal conversations. While 52% are regular users, with daily chatting among 13%, concerns arise regarding the impact of AI on teens' well-being. Boys are slightly more likely to have never used AI companions compared to girls. Teen usage for various purposes includes entertainment, social interaction, and advice, with half expressing distrust in the information provided. A significant number of teens find AI interactions satisfying and practice social skills through these conversations. Importantly, 80% of teens claim to spend more time with real friends than AI companions, indicating that technology is not replacing real-life relationships.
