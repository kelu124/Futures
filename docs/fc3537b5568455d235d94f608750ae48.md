## Summary

The article assesses the landscape of AI over the next two years, focusing on various pressures affecting its trajectory. It highlights a disconnect between AI investment and tangible enterprise results, suggesting that while adoption is accelerating, integrating AI into organizations remains complex. Revenue growth in the generative AI sector is significant, on par with rapid technological advancements witnessed in the past. Yet, energy constraints pose a significant challenge for scaling AI infrastructure, particularly in the US, where grid limitations may slow down ambitious growth initiatives. The author emphasizes that this overview is not predictive but aims to orient readers through a dynamic and evolving AI landscape.
