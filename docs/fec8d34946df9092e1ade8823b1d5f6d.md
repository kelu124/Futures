## Summary

The text follows a New York college student who becomes a mystery shopper to earn extra money. Posing as various customers, she evaluates businesses while grappling with the ethical implications of her role, which can lead to employees losing their jobs. The narrative explores the morality of corporate surveillance and the power dynamics between mystery shoppers and underpaid workers. As she completes assignments, she feels increasingly uncomfortable with her actions and their potential consequences, culminating in a realization of her own disempowerment within the capitalist system. Ultimately, she decides to seek other means of income.
